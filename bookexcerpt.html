We only saw these monkeys around entrances that had structures.

But baboons were out in the bush and they weren't afraid of much either.

Here's an excerpt from the book ...
At a point near the airport, we stopped near a watering hole based on a rumor that a lion was nearby. Things seemed quiet while we watched a lone warthog cautiously approach the pond for a drink. All was calm and serene when out of nowhere, a female lion leaps out of the bush and just misses the warthog. We watched the warthog run right by our vehicle close enough to see the "holy shit" look on his face. It would have been way cool if the lioness was still chasing him, but she gave up. Our driver, Gillead, did his best impression of the warthog, "Oh Jesus, Oh Jesus. I'll never drink water again. And I'm gonna be a much better hog from now on."

An encounter with one of these is no laughing matter.

Jackals appeared to be fearless. I'm not sure what preys on them, but I bet it's a lot of work.

Made a bad joke about not being able to get close to this guy. It's because we were ostrichized.
